# Global Sales Transactions Dataset

## About the project

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of the Global Sales Transactions Dataset using Microsoft Excel. The objective was to clean, transform, analyze, and visualize global sales data to uncover meaningful business insights related to revenue, profit, cost, product performance, regions, sales channels, and order priorities.

The final output is an interactive Excel dashboard that enables users to explore sales performance across multiple dimensions such as item type, region, year, sales channel, and order priority. This project demonstrates strong skills in data cleaning, pivot table analysis, KPI design, and dashboard storytelling using Excel.

## Project Objective 

The objective of this project was to use Microsoft Excel to clean, analyze, and visualize global sales transaction data in order to understand revenue, profit, product performance, regional trends, sales channel effectiveness and create an interactive dashboard.
## Tools
Excel
## Steps
1. Load the data into Excel
2. Clean and prepare raw sales data using Excel: By handling inconsistencies, formatting variables, and ensuring data accuracy for analysis.
3. Calculate key business metrics (KPI's) such as revenue, cost, profit, and profit margin

4. Analyze sales performance by product type and region

5. Compare online and offline sales channels: To identify performance changes and potential business: To be able to identify high- and low-performing product categories. risks.

6. Identify high profit products and priority orders

7. Build an interactive Excel dashboard for easy decision making

## Key Insights

a. The business is highly profitable, generating approximately GHS 3.9B in total profit from GHS 13.1B in revenue and aprofit margin of about 30% indicates efficient cost management across operations.

b. Among all the product in K_Bissco Supper Market, Cosmetics, Household, and Office Supplies are the top profit generating item types, contributing the largest share of total profit. In contrast, Beverages and Fruits generate relatively lower revenue and profit, suggesting either lower margins or weaker demand.

c. Offline Sales is about 50.02% and Online Sales is about 49.98%. This balance suggests that both channels are equally important to the business and should be strategically maintained.

d. The downward profit trend observed from 2015 through 2017 and the Peak profitability occurred around 2014-2015 period shows stabilization at lower profit levels, which requires strategic intervention.

## Recommendations:

i. Increase marketing and supply investment in Cosmetics, Household, and Office Supplies, as they deliver the highest returns.

ii.  Maintain the balanced online and offline strategy, while exploring digital enhancements to slightly push online growth.

iii. Conduct deeper analysis on seasonal trends and promotional impact
